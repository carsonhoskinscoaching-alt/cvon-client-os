Rate of Change
2
What Did the Scale Do?
Trainerize
v

Pull average weight from Trainerize. Compare to last week. Calculate ROC as % of bodyweight. Week 1 -- never make adjustments regardless of the number.

✓ On Target ROC

Above 20% BF: 1%/wk. 15-20%: 0.75%/wk. 12-15%: 0.5%/wk. Below 12%: 0.35%/wk. System working. Confirm consistency in Step 3.

! Progress Slow -- Week 1 of Slowdown

One slow week is noise. Do not adjust. Check consistency.

X Slow or Stalled -- 2 Consecutive Weeks

Two weeks is your trigger. Go to Step 3 to determine if it is a consistency problem or true plateau.

Consistency Check
3
Were They Consistent Across All Variables?
Form + Trainerize
v
Consistency Thresholds -- ALL must be met to call clean data
  • Calories: 5/7 days within 100 of target. Hit = within 100 over OR within 200 under. Any single day 300+ over is an automatic flag.
  • Protein: 5/7 days at goal or within 10g under.
  • Logging: Everything logged every day. One day with missing items makes the calorie number meaningless.
  • Steps: 5/7 days at step goal.
  • Workouts: 3/3. Binary. 2 out of 3 is a miss.
✓ All Thresholds Met

Data is clean. If on track: system working, acknowledge and keep moving. If off for 2 consecutive weeks with clean data: true plateau, go to Step 5.

Loom DirectionYou were locked in this week. Everything you controlled, you controlled. That is exactly what this looks like when it is working. Same targets, keep building.
X One or More Thresholds Missed

Data is not clean. Do not adjust targets. Go to Step 4 to find the break.

4
Where Did the System Break?
Form Follow-Ups
v

The form answers tell you what broke and why. Read for root cause before you record the Loom. You deliver answers -- not questions.

IF FORM IS THIN ON DETAIL -- Still deliver the check-in. Coach what Trainerize shows. Then say once in the Loom: "I don't have enough detail this week to give you the feedback you deserve. Next check-in I need more from you." Say it once, hold the standard, move on.
Calories Missed

Over 300+ in a day: something broke socially or emotionally. Fix = pre-plan high-risk situations.

Under 200+ below target: not a bonus. Sets up compensate-later pattern. Fix = eat consistently.

Weekend overages: fix is pre-logging Saturday and Sunday meals the night before -- never drop weekend targets.

Logging Incomplete

If not in the log, the number is meaningless. Fix = log everything in real time including estimates. If a meal was missed, log it with best estimate next time app is opened. No skipping days.

Steps Missed

Almost always a scheduling problem. Fix = schedule dedicated 30-40 min walks at specific times (before work, lunch, after work). Not vague walk-more advice.

Alcohol / Social Situations

Rule: max 2-3 drinks. Lighter options (light beer, spirits with soda water, wine) so it fits the budget. Everything consumed must be logged including drinks -- use AI calorie estimates if needed. Food eaten out also fits the budget.

Workout Missed

Identify which specific day was missed. If recurring, restructure the 3 training days around their real schedule.

Lift Plateau (Form Q10)
  • Single lift stuck 3 weeks: swap variation or shift rep range
  • Multiple lifts stuck 2-3 weeks: consider a refeed day
  • Strength dropping across all lifts: deficit likely too deep

After finding the break:

  • Pick ONE thing to fix. Not three. One.
  • Make it specific and non-negotiable.
  • Write it in notes before closing out.
Adjustment
5
Do We Need to Make an Adjustment?
Decision
v

Only reach this step if all thresholds met for 2 consecutive weeks and progress still off. Never adjust in Week 1. Never adjust both calories and steps in the same week.

X Completely Stalled -- No Loss 2 Weeks

Bigger move. Pick one: drop 150-200 cals OR add 2K steps/day. Choose based on what is more sustainable for this client.

Loom DirectionYou have been locked in for two weeks and the scale has not moved. That is your body adapting. We are making a move -- [state the adjustment]. Everything else stays the same. Give it two weeks.
! Below Target ROC but Still Losing

Smaller nudge. Pretty close to target: drop 75-100 cals or add 1,000-1,500 steps. Significantly below for 2 weeks: drop 100-150 cals or add 1,500-2,000 steps. Use judgment on which variable fits this client better.

✓ Above Target ROC

Usually leave it. Only intervene if significantly above for multiple weeks AND strength is dropping. Add 100 cals back and reassess.

X Inconsistent -- Do Not Adjust
Loom DirectionI am not changing your targets this week because we do not have clean data yet. When you are hitting everything consistently and the scale still is not moving, that is when we make a move. Right now we fix the system.
